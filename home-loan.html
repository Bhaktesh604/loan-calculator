Credit Market in India: An Overview of Loan Trends and EMI Calculators

The credit market in India is experiencing steady growth. Currently, India ranks as the 4th largest credit industry globally, with a remarkable Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of over 11% year on year. A major chunk of this growth is driven by short-term credit products such as personal loans and credit cards, which together make up 78% of all credit lending in the country. Understanding how to manage loan repayments, particularly the Equated Monthly Installment (EMI), is essential for effective financial planning.

Key Factors Affecting Your Loan EMI Amount

Several factors determine the amount of EMI you need to repay for your loan. By understanding these factors, you can better plan for your financial commitments:

  • Loan Tenure: Your loan's tenure may impact the EMI amount. A shorter tenure results in higher EMIs, while a longer tenure leads to lower monthly payments.
  • Interest Rate: The rate of interest directly affects your EMI. A lower interest rate results in a more affordable EMI.
  • Loan Amount: The total loan amount influences your EMI calculation. Higher loan amounts lead to higher EMIs, while smaller amounts have lower monthly payments.

EMI Calculation Formula

The EMI amount is calculated using the formula:

EMI = [P × R × (1 + R)^N] / [(1 + R)^(N - 1)]

Where:

  • P: Principal loan amount
  • R: Monthly rate of interest
  • N: Loan tenure (in months)

This standardized formula is used by online EMI calculators to provide accurate estimates of your monthly payments.
